## Defects

- `q606`: English true/false prompt says `protects the right ... and
  protection from discrimination`, and the Swedish stem similarly joins
  `skyddar rätten` with `skydd mot`. Both need a parallel construction such as
  protecting the right to practise religion and protection against
  discrimination.
- `q607`: English false-statement prompt says `protects that the state chooses
  a religion`, and the Swedish stem says `skyddar att staten väljer religion`.
  Both are learner-visible generated grammar defects.
- `q611`: false answer is debatable as written. UHR says many Swedes celebrate
  Christian holidays such as Christmas and Easter even if they are not
  religious, and separately describes Eid al-Fitr/Newroz as newer traditions
  brought to Sweden. It does not support the app treating `Many Swedes
  celebrate Eid al-Fitr and Newroz...` as clearly false.
- `q622`: true-statement prompt says Jews gained the right to live `in the
  country` / `i landet`, which is referential instead of standalone. It should
  name Sweden in both languages.
