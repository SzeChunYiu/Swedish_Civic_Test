# Norway citizenship test research note

Date researched: 2026-05-18
Lane: MANAGER-research
Atom: `nordic:norway citizenship/residence test - structure, topics, sources`

## Executive summary

Norway separates citizenship readiness into a civic-knowledge test path and a
spoken-language requirement. For citizenship applicants aged 18-67, UDI says the
applicant must have passed an oral Norwegian test at B1 or higher and either
the citizenship test or the social studies test in Norwegian. The citizenship
test is positioned as the lower-language civic option: HK-dir lists it at A2,
while the social studies test covers the same curriculum at a higher language
level.

For Swedish Civic Test, the strongest transferable lesson is not item content.
It is the product model: a clearly scoped civic curriculum, plain-language
question style, three-option single-answer questions, explicit pass threshold,
and topic coverage that blends democracy, rights, work, school, family, health,
everyday life, history, diversity, and digital judgement.

## Curriculum coverage

The official social studies curriculum behind the test emphasizes:

- Democratic participation, governance, public institutions, working-life
  cooperation, social trust, and legitimacy of democratic decisions.
- Individual/community links across family, welfare state, education, work,
  voluntary sector, sustainability, and everyday social arenas.
- Critical thinking, source evaluation, digital judgement, and comparing Norway
  with societies participants already know.
- Three named modules: education/competence/working life; family/health/everyday
  life; Norway before and now.
- Competence aims including education rights and duties, employee rights and
  obligations, tax-funded welfare, privacy and copyright, child and youth legal
  protection, public services supporting families, access to health services,
  immigrant rights and obligations, active participation in social/voluntary/
  political arenas, negative social control and related law/support, historical
  events behind democracy, holidays, separation of powers, parties and elections,
  diversity including the Sami, sustainability, gender equality, human rights,
  freedom of expression, discrimination protection, and religion/tradition over
  time.

## Transferable design implications for Swedish Civic Test

1. Use a curriculum-first coverage map. Norway's test page points candidates to
   a defined social studies curriculum rather than a hidden bank. Swedish
   question sets should map each item to a UHR/source-based civic competence,
   not just a factual topic.
2. Keep language plain even when the underlying subject is legal or historical.
   Norway's citizenship test is explicitly the lower-language route, which
   supports our existing naturalness and translation-complete work.
3. Prefer practical civic scenarios, not trivia-only prompts. Norway's
   curriculum repeatedly frames knowledge as rights, obligations, access to
   services, participation, and everyday decision-making.
4. Keep one correct answer with plausible but clearly wrong distractors. Norway
   uses three answer options and one correct answer, which is simpler than many
   four-option trivia formats and may improve mobile ergonomics.
5. Separate exam simulation from official-material study. Norway warns that its
   example test is not the real test. Our app should keep the same boundary:
   practice format and coverage, not claims of official replication.

## Sweden content-enrichment opportunities

These are research proposals for later CONTENT/VALIDATOR routing, not direct
data edits:

- Build a coverage matrix that maps Swedish questions to civic competencies:
  democracy/constitution, public institutions, courts and rights, school and
  child welfare, work and taxes, health and social insurance, migration and
  citizenship process, equality and discrimination, national minorities,
  environment/sustainability, and source/digital judgement.
- Audit whether each chapter has enough practical "what should a resident do?"
  questions, for example contacting the right public service, understanding a
  right/obligation, or identifying a democratic participation route.
- Consider a three-option practice mode for beginner Swedish readers, while
  preserving the existing app model if four-option questions are already an
  accepted product requirement.
