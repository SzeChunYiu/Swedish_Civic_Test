# Finland citizenship and language requirement research note

Date researched: 2026-05-20
Lane: RESEARCH
Atom: `nordic:finland citizenship/language requirement - structure, sources`

## Executive summary

Finland is a useful Nordic contrast because, as of 2026-05-20, its live
citizenship application path is still built around language proof rather than
a civic-knowledge citizenship test. Migri requires adult citizenship applicants
to show at least satisfactory oral and written skills in Finnish or Swedish,
or equivalent sign-language skills where applicable, and to hold the language
certificate before submitting the citizenship application.

At the same time, Finland is actively moving toward a civic-knowledge test.
The Finnish Government submitted a proposal to Parliament on 2026-04-16 to add
civic knowledge as a citizenship requirement. The proposed test would be
introduced after the legislative amendments enter into force at the beginning
of 2027. Because that proposal is not the current application route, Swedish
Civic Test should treat Finland as a reform case: learn from its planned
curriculum and transparency model, but do not describe Finland as already
operating a citizenship-test bank.

## Curriculum and topic pattern

Finland's current citizenship path is language-forward. It tests integration
primarily through proven ability to use Finnish or Swedish in official and
educational contexts, not through a standalone society quiz.

The proposed 2027 civic-knowledge test has a narrower planned topic set than
a full study book but a strong rights-and-institutions core:

- How Finnish society works and its key principles.
- Key legislation on Finnish society.
- Fundamental and human rights.
- Equality and gender equality.
- Finland's history and culture.
- Public learning materials prepared in advance for transparency and
  impartiality.

## Transferable design implications for Swedish Civic Test

1. Separate language support from civic knowledge. Finland's current model
   makes clear that language proof and civic knowledge are different policy
   instruments. Swedish Civic Test can support bilingual learning without
   pretending to certify Swedish-language ability.
2. Mark reform-stage facts by date. Finland's planned test is legally and
   operationally time-bound: proposed on 2026-04-16, intended for introduction
   after amendments enter into force in early 2027. Time-sensitive reform
   content should carry a review date.
3. Prefer public study-material alignment. Finland's proposal explicitly
   points to predefined public learning material. That reinforces our UHR-first
   approach and argues against hidden-bank style content.
4. Keep rights and equality central. The proposed Finnish curriculum foregrounds
   legislation, human rights, equality, and gender equality, which are also
   important Swedish civic-prep domains.
5. Avoid false currentness. A product should not market practice questions as
   mirroring a Finnish citizenship test before the test is actually in force
   and official format details are final.
