## Executive summary

Iceland is a useful Nordic contrast because its citizenship route is
language-test centred, not civic-knowledge-test centred. Official Government
of Iceland material points citizenship applicants to the Directorate of
Immigration and the Icelandic Nationality Act. The current practical test
surface is the Icelandic language test for citizenship, not a separate society
or history quiz.

For Swedish Civic Test, the main lesson is product boundary discipline. A civic
prep app can support language learning through audio, bilingual explanations,
and plain Swedish, but it should not imply that practice questions certify
Swedish-language ability. Iceland also reinforces the value of a visible
learner-facing language level: its citizenship language test is described as
A2-level Icelandic for foreigners/as a second language.

## Curriculum and topic pattern

Iceland does not appear, from the official sources checked in this iteration,
to run a separate citizenship civic-knowledge exam comparable to Norway's
citizenship/social-studies test or Denmark's citizenship/residence tests.

The tested integration skill is practical Icelandic language use:

- understanding and participating in familiar daily situations;
- simple conversations and discussions about familiar topics;
- reading and writing short simple texts;
- listening for main topics in familiar radio/television material;
- four-domain language assessment: listening, reading, writing, speaking.

## Transferable design implications for Swedish Civic Test

1. Keep language support separate from language certification. Iceland's
   citizenship test is explicitly a language test; this app's Swedish audio and
   English support should be framed as study aids, not proof of Swedish
   proficiency.
2. Make learner-facing language levels explicit when possible. Iceland's A2
   label helps learners understand difficulty. Swedish civic questions can use
   plain-language labels or easy-read modes without implying an official CEFR
   certification.
3. Preserve official-process humility. Iceland's public guidance routes
   applicants to the Directorate of Immigration and legal rules. Swedish copy
   should continue pointing learners to UHR and Migrationsverket for current
   requirements.
4. Treat legal/application details as volatile. Exemption and conduct rules
   should not become quiz facts unless verified from an official current page
   and date-stamped.
5. Use language-accessibility as a product quality benchmark. A civic app for
   second-language learners should support listening, reading, and simple
   phrasing even when it does not test or certify speaking/writing.

## Sweden content-enrichment opportunities

These are research proposals only; no Icelandic question content should be
copied or adapted.

- Add a product guard that distinguishes "study language support" from
  "official Swedish-language requirement" wherever Settings/audio/onboarding
  copy describes English support or Swedish audio.
- When a future language-learning surface is added, label it as practice for
  comprehension and confidence, not as a substitute for any official language
  test.
- If Sweden's official language-test route changes, date-stamp the app's
  requirements copy separately from the UHR civic-knowledge question bank.
