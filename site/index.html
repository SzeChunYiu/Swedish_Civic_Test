Chapter 3 · Q47
Which Swedish principle gives everyone the right to walk, swim, and pick berries on most land in nature?

Allemansrätten — "everyone's right" — lets you walk, swim, ski, camp, and forage on most land in Sweden. The catch: be considerate, and don't pitch a tent in someone's flowerbed.

Frequently, honestly asked

Questions you'd ask over coffee.

Is this the official Swedish citizenship test?

No, and we say so on every page. We're independent — not affiliated with UHR, Skolverket, Migrationsverket, or anyone who actually decides who becomes a citizen. We make a study tool. They run the exam.

Will it really help me pass?

It'll help you study. The rest depends on you, the official source material, and how much fika you take. We pull questions from the public bank and explain each one in plain language.

Do I need an account?

No. The MVP needs zero registration. No email, no phone number, no awkward profile photo from 2017. Progress lives on your device.

Is it free?

Free to start, free to study, and free to take mock exams. Ads help keep the core app available; Remove Ads is an optional one-time 29 SEK purchase that removes ads.

Does it work in Swedish too?

Yes. Toggle the EN / SV switch in the nav, or change it inside the app. Many learners use both at once — read the question in Swedish, peek at the English when you're stuck.

Is my data shared with anyone?

Your study progress, answers, mistakes, and settings stay local. Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) in the app can process ad and consent signals. The website's Google AdSense slots stay disabled until reviewed web slot IDs are configured, and ads never receive your study answers or progress.

Independence

Almost Swedish is an independent study app for Swedish civic knowledge practice. It is not official and is not affiliated with UHR, Skolverket, Migrationsverket, or the Swedish government. Anything you read here represents what this app does — nothing more.

No account required

The MVP requires no account, email address, phone number, or profile registration. You open the app, you study, you close the app. That's the deal.

Local study progress

Study progress, settings, mistakes, XP, streaks, badges, bookmarks, and audio preferences are stored locally on your device. They never leave it. If you uninstall the app, that data is gone — fair warning.

Current data-collection posture

In plain Swedish: study progress and answers stay local; ad systems do not receive them.

We do not run account profiles or send study answers, mistakes, progress, settings, XP, streaks, badges, bookmarks, or audio preferences to ad providers. Google ad systems may process ad and consent signals as described below.

Ads and purchases

This website has Google AdSense-ready ad slots, but they stay disabled until reviewed slot IDs are configured. The mobile app uses Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) behind Google's consent flow. Remove Ads is an optional one-time 29 SEK purchase that removes ads. Ads help fund the free study experience, and ads never collect study answers or progress.

Ads on this website

This website is prepared for Google AdSense, but the static build does not load AdSense until reviewed web slot IDs are configured. When enabled, AdSense and its partners may set cookies on your device and use them to personalise ads, measure performance, and detect fraud. We load AdSense according to your cookie choice: Accept all allows personalised ads, while Necessary only keeps ads non-personalised. You can change your choice by clearing site data for this domain.

Ads in the mobile app

The mobile app uses Google Mobile Ads (AdMob). On first launch the app shows Google's official consent screen (via the User Messaging Platform SDK), where you can pick personalised, non-personalised, or — in regions where the choice is available — opt out. Ads keep the app free. We never use ads to collect your study answers or progress.

Changes & contact

If this policy changes, the effective date at the top of this page will change with it. Questions, concerns, or "wait, what does this actually mean?" — head over to the support page.

What we can help with

Email us if you find:

  • A content issue — a wrong answer, an outdated reference, a typo with attitude.
  • Confusing Swedish wording in a question or explanation.
  • A broken source link or missing citation.
  • An audio problem — clip cutting off, wrong pronunciation, silence where speech should be.
  • A study-flow bug — progress that didn't save, a streak that broke for no reason, an unfinishable mock exam.
  • A store or build issue — crash on launch, billing weirdness, a download that won't.

What to include

Help us help you faster:

  • Device + OS version (Pixel 7, Android 14 / iPhone 13, iOS 17).
  • App version (Settings → About).
  • What you did, what you expected, what happened instead.
  • A screenshot, if it's a visual bug.

Please don't include sensitive info

No personal data, please. Don't send government identifiers (personnummer, samordningsnummer), immigration case details, passport scans, financial info, or anything you wouldn't write on a postcard.

What we can't do

We can fix app functionality and content corrections. We can't:

  • Provide official exam answers or leaked content.
  • Give migration or legal advice.
  • Speak for any government agency.
  • Predict when your case will be decided. (Nobody can.)

Contact

Email support@medborgartest.app. We read everything. We answer almost everything. Privacy details live on the privacy page.

Current question source

  • UHR — Sverige i fokus — public study material for the Swedish citizenship test.
  • Every shipped question cites a chapter, section, and page from this material.
  • The public source list changes only when the shipped question bank contains questions with that visible provenance.

How citations work

  • Question cards show the source line below the question, separate from the stem.
  • The static question bank stores the same source title, chapter, section, and page for every row.

Future source families

  • Other public sources may be added later, but only after matching questions ship with visible citations.
  • This page is checked against site/questions.js so source copy cannot outrun the current bank.

Current-source scope

  • The bank is UHR-only today, based on Sverige i fokus.
  • The app is independent and is not an official UHR product.

Editorial method

House rule: if it can't be cited to a public source, it doesn't make the question bank.

Every question goes through draft → source-check → plain-language pass → native-Swedish review. When a source updates (a new election, a law amendment, a renamed agency), the affected questions get a refresh tag and re-enter review.

See a question that disagrees with its cited source? Tell us — corrections are usually live within a week.
